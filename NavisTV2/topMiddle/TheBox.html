Response to Bill Gates’ Review of Marc Levinson's “The Box”

Michelle Gaubert (MGaubert) / ‎2013-08-21 10:41 AM ‎2013-09-30 08:12 AM

“In the second half of the twentieth century, an innovation came along that would transform the way the world did business. At first, some people wrote it off as a fad. Others kept at it, convinced that it was going to have a huge impact.”
This is how Bill Gates’ opens up a blog post last week on The Gates Notes. Gates goes on to discuss the book “The Box: How the Shipping Container Made the World Smaller and the World Economy Bigger,” by Marc Levinson, a former Economist editor. While we may think that Gates is talking about some other technology with this introduction, he’s actually talking about the shipping industry – specifically the shipping container.

The book discusses the beginnings of container shipping and how it changed global trade and the global economy as a whole. The interesting point that Gates brings up though, is that this book also challenges commonly held views about business and the role that innovation plays. “For example, you often hear that it’s a big advantage to get into a particular business early. But in both software and shipping, that wasn’t necessarily the case,” writes Gates.

He goes on to describe the conditions that make it possible for innovation to take place, whether it be for the development of the container or not, as a lot of this can depend on government involvement. It’s unlikely that shipping companies in the 50s and 60s could raise enough capital without governmental support to invest in the industry as a whole, such as investing in new cranes or deepening the waterways.

The complicated process of innovation also holds true when it comes to setting standards, as it’s difficult to know where standards will come from, whether it be for the shipping container or the Internet. “Few people would have predicted that the Internet protocol would grow out of a university research project funded by the U.S. Department of Defense,” writes Gates.

Gates mentions that in the 1950s, few people understood how important the shipping container would be in shaping the global economy, and mentions that this is almost always the case with any type of innovation. “It’s hard to predict which ones will fizzle and which ones will change the world,” he writes.

The shipping container was a revolutionary innovation. What do you think will be the next big industry innovation that will be as impactful as the evolution of the shipping container?

We can speculate, but only time will tell. As Gates says, “You never know where the next shipping container will come from.”
