25 Years of Moving Terminals Forward

Bill Walsh (BWalsh) / ‎2013-09-26 11:14 AM ‎2013-09-26 11:14 AM

Looking back to 1988, it’s hard to believe how far we have come as a company. Offering the industry’s first terminal operating system (TOS) presents its fair share of pressures and challenges, but has also afforded us the advantage of setting the industry standard. Today, despite the many TOS options available to the market, we’re proud that top terminals around the world continue to select Navis as their TOS of choice.

Much of our success and continued innovation is due to our employees. They’re as dedicated now as they were in our early years, when unfinished doors were used for desks and air conditioning was something on our corporate “wish list.” Navis employees have also faced some unique experiences, such as earthquakes and tropical disease while on site with and sizes, requiring our team members to be displaced for extended periods of customers. We’ve also been involved with complex implementations of all shapes time to ensure critical systems function as promised.

Special credit is also due to our esteemed team of technologists. Growing our company from just two people in 1988 to 400 people today who provide engineering, services and technical support is a major achievement. With their expertise, we’ve delivered significant upgrades to our operating system over the years, ultimately transforming the way that container terminals operate globally.

Lastly, our customers deserve tremendous recognition. They have stood by us as we’ve overcome the complexities of implementations, tested new waters and invested technology to move terminals forward. It’s hard to believe that it was over 6 years ago when Lyttelton Port of Christchurch in New Zealand became the first SPARCS N4 site to go live. Through that trust, collaboration and partnership, I’m proud to report that SPARCS N4 technology is now live and/or currently being deployed at 117 sites in more than 50 countries around the world.

Today, we can confidently say that Navis has become the trusted industry standard to the container transportation industry across the globe. I’m proud to be part of a company that has achieved so much in the past 25 years, and I look forward to what our future will hold as the industry progresses, and as we move forward with it.
